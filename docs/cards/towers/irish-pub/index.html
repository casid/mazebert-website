Despite being abused by creeps and looters, the clock hanging upon the Irish Pub’s back wall still works. Currently, it reads 11:59. As the clock’s seconds hand ticks away the time ‘til midnight, the Irish Pub’s owner climbs atop his weathered wooden bar and raises his glass. His patrons, who have filled the bar to bursting, raise theirs as well.

Because nothing, not even a siege, can stop the Irish Pub’s patrons from celebrating their favorite holiday.

“3!” shouts the barkeep. “2 … 1 … Happy St. Paddy’s Day!”

The barkeep downs his green Guinesses in one hard go. His patrons do the same, cheering as they finish. The four-man band in the corner begins playing Irish tunes, and the patrons clap and dance and every single one of them sings along. Even those who do not know the words.

Outside the Irish Pub, the siege of Metropolis continues. But the St. Paddy’s Day partiers can no longer hear the battle, over the sound of their revelry.
